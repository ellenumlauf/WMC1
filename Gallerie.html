Ansel Adams

the master of black and white

He was born in 1902 in San Francisco and is especially known for his stunning black-and-white photographs of the national parks in the western United States. Together with his friend and fellow photographer Fred Archer, Adams developed his now legendary Zone System in the 1930s to control the exposure and tonal range of black-and-white films.
